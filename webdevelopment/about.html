About Me

My name is Erin Shawhan, and I am a college student at the University of Missouri - Columbia. I am from Kansas City, Missouri and live in downtown Columbia during the school year. I am planning to graduate in May 2025 with a bachelor's degree in Information Technology and a minor in Psychological Sciences. I am pursuing a number of engineering certificates, including Cyber Security, Information Systems and Technology, Media Technology and Design, and Web and Mobile Applications Development.

I currently work as a Student Research Assistant at the Mizzou Motion Analysis Center. Aside from academics, I am involved in a few organizations here at Mizzou. I am the current Vice President of both Develop[Her] and Upsilon Pi Epsilon. I am also a member of the Society of Women Engineers. I am interested in working in software development and cybersecurity, but I am open to exploring many of the various fields of IT.

I became interested in IT because I love finding creative ways to solve problems and I believe technology has the ability to make a positive impact in the world. I am passionate about increasing equity and accessibility in tech and uplifting the voices of underrepresented communities in computing. In my personal life, some hobbies I enjoy are crocheting and playing with my dog, Finn!
